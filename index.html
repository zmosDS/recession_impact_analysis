The Hidden Shape of Recessions

Industry Declines & Recoveries
1995–2024

You probably lived through at least one recession…
but could you recognize it just by the shape it left behind?

An explorable explanation by Jillian O’Neel, Zack Mosley, & Alex Hayslip

Recessions leave a mark on all of us.

Recessions appear as sudden dips in total employment, and most people have lived through at least one. These events are shared, memorable disruptions.

Which recession do you remember most?

However, no two recessions behave the same.

The recessions of 2001, 2008, and 2020 were caused by different events and produced different patterns of job loss. Some were slow burns; others were sudden free-falls.

Additionally, the economy is made of many industries.

Behind the overall national employment trend are dozens of industries, each with its own employment cycle.

Which industry is closest to yours or someone you know?

Industries experience recessions differently.

Your chosen industry may have collapsed in one recession, boomed through another, or struggled to bounce back. Its story isn’t the same as the broader economy.

Recovery reshapes industries.

After each recession ends, industries settle into new trajectories. Some reinvent themselves and grow beyond pre-recession levels; others never recover.
